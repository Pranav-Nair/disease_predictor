Eat a healthy and balanced diet that includes iodine-rich foods like seaweed, seafood, and iodized salt.
Avoid goitrogenic foods like soy, cabbage, broccoli, and kale, as they can interfere with thyroid function.
Exercise regularly to maintain a healthy weight and reduce your risk of developing obesity-related thyroid disorders.
Manage stress levels through relaxation techniques like meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises.
Avoid smoking and limit alcohol consumption as both can negatively affect thyroid function.
Get regular check-ups and screenings to detect any potential thyroid issues early on.
Use caution when taking any new medications, supplements, or herbal remedies and discuss potential side effects with your healthcare provider.
Protect your neck from radiation exposure, as radiation can damage the thyroid gland.
Manage any underlying health conditions that may increase your risk of thyroid disease, such as diabetes or autoimmune disorders.
Practice good sleep hygiene to ensure that you are getting adequate restorative sleep.Sleep deprivation can negatively impact thyroid function.
